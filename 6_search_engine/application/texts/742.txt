Oakie Boogie ( sometimes Okie Boogie ) is a Western swing dance song written by Johnny Tyler in 1947 . It is recognizable by its refrain : : ' ' When you do the Oakie Boogie , and do it Oklahoma style , ' ' : ' ' That mean old Oakie Boogie is bound to drive you wild . ' ' Jack Guthrie 's version ( Capitol 341 ) reached #3 on the charts in 1947 and is often included in the list of the first rock and roll songs . The singing of Oakie Boogie is the only performance by Guthrie in a film -- Ernest Tubb 's ' ' Hollywood Barn Dance ' ' in 1947 . Ella Mae Morse also recorded a version for Capitol which reached #23 in 1952 . Her version was one of the first songs arranged by Nelson Riddle . Speedy West played pedal steel guitar on the recording . The song has been recorded by many artists over the years .
