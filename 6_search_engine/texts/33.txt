751514 ' ' The Black Camel ' ' ( 1929 ) is the fourth of the Charlie Chan novels by Earl Derr Biggers. # Plot summary # It tells the story of a Hollywood star ( Shelah Fane ) , who is stopping in Hawaii after she finished shooting a film on location in Tahiti . She is murdered in the pavilion of her renter house in Waikiki during her stay . The story behind her murder is linked with the three-year-old murder of another Hollywood actor and also connected with an enigmatic psychic named Tarneverro . Chan , in his position as a detective with the Honolulu Police Department , investigates amid public clamor demanding that the murderer be found and punished immediately . Death is a black camel that kneels unbidden at every gate . Tonight black camel has knelt here , Chan tells the suspects . # Film , TV or theatrical adaptations # It was adapted into a film of the same name based on the book and released in 1931 .
