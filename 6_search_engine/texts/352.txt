10997514 Bank of Bombay was the second of the three presidency banks ( others being the Bank of Calcutta and the Bank of Madras ) of the Raj period . It was established , pursuant to a charter of the British East India Company , on 15th April , 1840 . The bank was headquartered in Bombay , now called Mumbai . The Bank of Bombay undertook all the normal activities which a commercial bank was expected to undertake . The Bank of Madras , in the absence of any central banking authority at that time , also conducted certain functions which are ordinarily a preserve of a central bank . The Bank of Bombay , and two other Presidency banks , namely , the Bank of Calcutta and the Bank of Madras were amalgamated and the reorganized banking entity was named the Imperial Bank of India on 27 January 1921 .
