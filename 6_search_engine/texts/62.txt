1512514 The Tlikakila River ( Dena'ina Athabascan iq'a Qilanhtnu , literally salmon-are-there river ) is a stream , 51 mi long , in the U.S. state of Alaska . The river , lying entirely within Lake Clark National Park , flows southwest from Summit Lake in the Chigmit Mountains of the Aleutian Range to Lake Clark . The Tlikakila is one of three national wild rivers in Lake Clark National Park . The other two are the Mulchatna and the Chilikadrotna , all added in 1980 to the National Wild and Scenic Rivers System . # Boating # Varying from Class I ( easy ) to III ( difficult ) on the International Scale of River Difficulty , the Tlikakila River is floatable by raft and kayak . ' ' The Alaska River Guide ' ' describes it as suitable for intermediate boaters with good wilderness skills . To reach the floatable parts of the river from Summit Lake , boaters must make a 2mikm 0 portage .
