39230514 The Whalers Cabin near Carmel , California is a historic building that is listed on the National Register of Historic Places . It is located in what is now Point Lobos State Reserve , four miles south of Carmel . The cabin was built in the 1850s to house Japanese and Chinese fishermen . Shore whaling was conducted here by the Carmel Whaling Company from 1862 to 1884 and by the Japanese Whaling Company from 1898 to 1900 . The building now houses a museum dedicated to cultural history of the area . The museum also highlights the history of Point Lobos , including its cinematic appearances and plans at the turn of the 20th century to develop the area for densely packed suburban housing . It was listed on the National Register in 2007 ; the listing included one contributing building and one contributing site . # References #
